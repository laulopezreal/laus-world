We mainly use the adjective ‘cross’ (= ‘angry’) when talking to or about children. 
In a ‘heated’ argument, people allow their emotions to show
If you ‘**get worked up**’, you become angry over a period of time
We mainly use the adjective ‘**irate**’ (= ‘very angry’) to describe formal situations, like a customer who is angry (but still polite) after receiving bad service
An ‘**outburst**’ of temper is when somebody expresses their anger suddenly and strongly. 
If you’re ‘seething with anger’, you are very angry but try not to show it.
